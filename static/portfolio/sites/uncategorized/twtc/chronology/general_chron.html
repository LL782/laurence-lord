A Selective Libertion Chronology, 1945 to 1962.
1949

December 14: Israeli government moves capitol from Tel Aviv to Jerusalem, disregarding United Nations resolution for internationalisation of Jerusalem.

December 27: Netherlands transfers sovereignty of Indonesia to United States.
1950

January 29: In South Africa, first of series of riots in Johannesburg provoked by government racial policy.

June 25: North Korean forces invade South Korea; UN Security Council passes resolution demanding withdrawal of North Korean forces.
1952

July 23: General Muhammad Neguib seizes power in Egypt.

October 20: State of emergency proclaimed in Kenya because of Mau Mau (nationalist) disturbances; leading members of the Kenyan African Union are arrested.
1953

June 18 Republic proclaimed in Egypt, with General Neguib as president and prime minister, and Colonel Abdel Nasser as deputy prime minister. July 27 Korean armistice is signed at Panmunjom. October 6 Fearing communism in British Guiana (now Guyana), by the People's Progressive Party, Britain sends troops; the constitution is suspended. October 29 In Vietnam, French airborne troops capture the village of Dien Bien Phu.

1954

March 13 In Vietnam, Vietminh begin siege of French forces at Dien Bien Phu. April 18 General Nasser becomes Prime Minister and military governor of Egypt. June 15 In Gold Coast (now Ghana), Convention People's Party wins elections (Dr Kwame Nkrumah forms government). July 20 Armistice for Indochina signed in Geneva by which France evacuated North Vietnam. Ho Chi Minh forms government in North Vietnam. October 30 Insurrection in Algeria; the rebels call themselves the Front for National Liberation (FLN) and call for the establishment of an independent Algeria.

1955

April 18 Afro-Asian Conference held in Bandung, Indonesia, at invitation of President Sukharno: ministers of 29 states meet to form 'non-aligned bloc' of countries opposed to the 'imperialism' and 'colonialism' of the superpowers.

1956

June 26 President Nasser announces nationalisation of the Suez Canal. October, with covert support by the British and French governments, Israel invades Egypt. British and French planes bomb Egyptian airfields. November British paratroopers land at Port Said in Egypt. Following a UN resolution British withdraw and a UN emergency force arrives. December Fidel Castro and followers land in Cuba; they begin a guerrilla war.

1957

January 22 Israeli forces withdraw from Egypt but remain in ' Gaza Strip'.

1958

September 19 Cairo. Algerian rebel leader Ferhat Abbas proclaims establishment of Provisional Government of the Republic of Algeria. December 8 All-Africa People's Conference held in Accra, Ghana.

1959

January 1 - 3 Cuba. The guerrilla campaign of 26 July Movement forces President Batistá to resign and flee. Fidel Castro becomes Prime Minister.

1960

June 30 Belgium Congo becomes independent as Congo Republic with Patrice Lumumba as Prime Minister. He is killed in 1961 and in 1975 a US Senate report reveals CIA involvement. March 21 South Africa. 'Sharpeville massacre' by police during a demonstration by the Pan-African Congress against pass laws. December In North Vietnam the formation of the National Front for the Liberation of South Vietnam and the National Liberation Army, known as the Vietcong.

1961

February 4 - 5 Nationalist disturbances in Luanda, in the Portuguese territory of Angola.

1962

July 3 France proclaims independence of Algeria. August South Africa. The leader of Umkonto we Sizwe, Nelson Mandela, is arrested. October 22 Start of the 'Cuban Missile Crisis', the USA starts a naval blockade of Cuba in response to evidence of USSR missiles on the island.
