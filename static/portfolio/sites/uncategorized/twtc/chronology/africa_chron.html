A Selective Chronology of Africa and African Cinema
1945-1995
1959

First Television service is started in Africa, at Ibadan, Nigeria, while South Africa rejects the introduction of Television.
1960

French-governed Cameroon becomes the independent Republic of Cameroon.

British Prime Minister Macmillan makes 'Winds of Change' speech in Cape Town, South Africa.

Worst recorded earthquake in Africa: Agadir, Morocco - 12,000 are killed.

'Sharpeville Massacre' in South Africa.

ANC and PAC banned.

French-governed Togoland becomes independent Republic of Togoland.

Belgian-governed Congo becomes independent Congo Republic (now Zaire). There is internal strife and UN emergency forces are sent in.

Independence for Republics of Dahomey (now Benin), Niger, Upper Volta (now Burkina Faso), Ivory Coast, Chad and Central Africa (now Central African Republic).

1961

Rwandan provisional government proclaims independence and is placed under UN trusteeship.

Frantz Fanon publishes 'The Wretched of the Earth'.

Tanzania becomes an independent state within the Commonwealth.
1962

Independence for Algeria, Provisional Government returns from exile.

Uganda becomes independent within the Commonwealth.

Tanzania becomes a republic within the Commonwealth.
1963

Organisation of African Unity founded in a conference at Addis Ababa, Ethiopia.

Nigeria becomes a republic within the Commonwealth.

Kenya becomes independent within the Commonwealth.

Dissolution of the Central African Federation of Rhodesia and Nyasaland (now Zambia, Zimbabwe and Malawi).

In Senegal, Ousmane Sembène films 'Borom Sarret'.
1964

Palestine Liberation Organisation founded.

Nyasaland Protectorate becomes independent as Malawi.

Northern Rhodesia, renamed Zambia, becomes independent republic.

Chinua Achebe published 'Arrow of God'.
1965

Gambia becomes independent within the Commonwealth.

Bechuanaland (now Botswana) becomes self-governing.

Rhodesia declares illegal independence.

'The Battle of Algiers' made in Algeria with Italian director, Gillo Pontecorvo.
1966

President Nkrumah overthrown by military coup.
1967

Secession of Biafra provokes civil war in Nigeria.

Six day war between Israel and the Arab States.
1968

Conflict in Rhodesia between the Settler Regime and African Nationalist forces.
1969

Julius Nyere publishes 'Freedom and Socialism'.

Athol Fugard's play 'Boesman and Lena'.

1970

Gambia becomes a republic within the Commonwealth.

Med Hondo, Mauritania films 'Soleil O' in exile in France.
1971

Idi Amin seizes power in Uganda.
1972

Ugandan Asians given 90 days to leave the country by President Amin.
1973

December: 'Third World Filmmakers Meeting' in Algiers.

Youssef Chahine films 'Al-'usfur' ['The Sparrow'] in Egypt.
1974

Military coup deposes Emperor Haile Selassie in Ethiopia.

'Rumble in the Jungle' in Kinshasa, Zaire; Muhammad Ali beats George Foreman.
1975

Mozambique becomes independent after liberation war.

Independence in Angola, but civil war breaks out.

Wole Soyinka publishes 'Death and the King's Horseman'.
1976

Haile Gerima, Ethiopia, films 'Mirt sost shi amir' ['Harvest 3,000 Years'].
1977

Djibouti gains independence from France.

Ousmane Sembène films 'Ceddo' in Senegal.
1978

Israel invades southern Lebanon.
1979

End of UDI in Rhodesia.
1980

Rhodesia gains independence as Zimbabwe.
1981

UNESCO publish 'General History of Africa'.

Michel Khleifi of Palestine, films 'Al-dhrikrayat al-khasibah' ['Fertile Memories'].
1982

Rebel forces in Chad overthrow the President.

Over 800 Palestinians killed in West Beirut refugee camps.

March: 'Niamey Manifesto of African Filmmakers' issued.

Soulemane Cissé, Mali, films 'Finye' ['The Wind'].
1983

South Africa imposes illegal rule over South-West Africa [Namibia].
1984

Upper Volta renamed Burkina Faso.
1985

Major famine in Ethiopia.

'Live Aid' concert.

State of Emergency in South Africa.
1988

'Mapantsula' released in South Africa, then restricted to video by Apartheid Government.
1993

Apartheid Government begins process of dismantling apartheid and the introduction of black majority rule.
