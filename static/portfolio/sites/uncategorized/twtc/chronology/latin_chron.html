A Selective Chronology of Latin America and its Cinema, 1945 to 1995.
1946

February: Juan Perón elected president of Argentina.
1948

Foundation of the Organization of American States [OAS] at the 9th Pan-American Conference at Bogotá, Columbia. Military junta takes over government in Peru.
1952

In Cuba, Batistá (in exile since 1944) overthrows the government. In Argentina, death from cancer of Eva ('Evita') Perón, wife of President Juan Perón.
1953

In Cuba, Fidel Castro leads a failed attempt to overthrow Batista government.

1954

Montevideo Film Festival launched.Opening meeting of Organization of American States in Caracas, Venezuela, adopts US motion denouncing international communism as a threat to 'the sovereignty and political independence of the Americas'.
1955

Rising in Córdoba spreads throughout Argentina. Juan Perón resigns, a General Lonardi assumes presidency. Cuban filmmaker Julio García Espinosa produced the clandestine 'El Megano', a portrait of the charcoal burners in the Zapata Swamps.It is seized by the secret police.
1956

Fidel Castro and followers land in Cuba and begin guerilla war.
1958

'Tire dié' ['Throw me a Dime']; first important documentary by Fernando Birri in Santa Fé, Argentina.
1959

In Cuba, the guerilla campaign of the 26 July Movement forces Batista to flee. Fidel Castro becomes Prime Minister. Transfer of land from large landowners to those who work it. Cuban government expropriates US-owned sugar mills and plantations. Instituto Cubano de Arte e Industria Cinematográficos - ICAIC (Cuban Institute of Cinematographic Art and Industry) founded.
1961

USA suspends purchases of sugar from Cuba.
1961

USA severs diplomatic relations with Cuba. 1,500 Cuban exiles, trained by US military instructors, invade Cuba; an expected rising fails to occur and the invaders are killed or captured.

1962

Cuban missile 'crisis', withdrawal of 'offensive weapons'. Glauber Rocha's first feature 'Barravento' ['The Turning Wind'] marks the beginning of 'Cinema Nôvo' in Brazil.
1964

Anti-US riots in Panama, which breaks off diplomatic relations. Military coup in Brazil deposes President.
1967

Revolutionary Che Guevara executed in Bolivia. Filmmakers meeting at Viña del Mar, Chile adopts name of 'Nuevo Cine Latinoamericano' ['New Latin American Cinema'].
1968

President in Brazil emergency powers to avert left-wing coup. Fernando Solanas and Octavio Getino complete 'La hora de los hornos' ['The Hour of the Furnaces'], it is circulated secretly. In Bolivia Ukamau, including Jorge Sanjinés, produce 'Yawar Malku' ['Blood of the Condor'], it leads to ejecting of US 'Peace Corp'.
1969

Military junta takes power in Brazil. Rocha completes his fourth film 'O Dragão da maldade cointra o santo querreiro' ['Antõnio das Mortes']; then he goes into exile. Solanas and Getino publish 'Towards a Third Cinema'.
1970

Salvador Allende wins Chilean Presidential election.
1971

Left-wing Tupamaros guerrillas kidnap the British Ambassador to Uruguay.
1973

Perónist candidate wins Argentinian general election and in July resigns to make way for General Perón. Military junta, headed by General Pinochet, seize power in Chile: over 2500 die in fighting.
1974

Isabel Perón becomes president of Argentinian on the death of her husband.
1975

An interim US Senate report reveals CIA had plotted to kill foreign leaders, including Castro.
1976

Argentina suspends diplomatic ties with Britain over the Malvinas / Falkland Islands. Military coup deposes President Isabel Perón of Argentina; all political parties and unions are 'suspended'. Solanas and Getino [among others] go into exile.
1977

USA and Panama sign the Panama Canal Treaty which returns the canal zone to Panama.
1978

General strike in Nicaragua.
1979

Anastasio Somoza, dictator of Nicaragua, flees to the USA. Sandinista rebels set up new government. 'La batalla de Chile: La lucha de un pueblo sin armas: El poder popular' ['The Battle of Chile, part 3'] completes the record of the Chilean counter-revolution, Patricio Guzmán and his colleagues were assisted by the ICAIC and Chris Marker.
1980

Archbishop Romero shot dead while celebrating mass, San Salvador. End of 12 years of military rule in Peru.
1981

US accuses USSR and Cuba of attempting to subvert El Salvador.
1982

Argentine troops 'invade' Malvinas / Falkland Islands. British Royal Navy task Force sails. In June, Argentine forces surrender at Port Stanley, ending the war. In August, Argentine air- force and navy commanders resign from military junta. Government lifts ban on political parties.
1983

President Reagan of USA declares support for aim of Nicaraguan Contras to overthrow Sandinista government. 40,000 Argentines protest at proposed amnesty for military personel involved in human-rights violations in the 1970s. Radical party gains absolute majority in election. Héctor Olivera makes a black comedy 'No habrá más penas ni olvido' ['A Funny, Dirty Little War'] about Peronist militants in 1970s.
1984

International Court of Justice in the Hague rules that the USA should cease blockade of Nicaraguan ports. Sandinista Front wins Nicaraguan elections. 'Camilla' filmed by Maria Luisa Bemberg, Argentina.
1985

Julio Sanguinetti takes office as Uruguay's first elected president in twelve years. Military rule ends in Brazil. USA imposes financial and trade sanctions on Nicaragua.
1986

Presidents of Nicaragua, Guatemala, El Salvador. Honduras and Costa Rica endorse peace treaty and call for end to US military intervention in area. In November, US national security Adviser dismissed after revelations that money from arms sales was channelled to Contra rebels in Nicaragua. Luis Puenzo's 'La historia official' ['The Official Version'] dramatises the disappeared children under the junta.
1988

Nicaragua expels US ambassador and seven colleagues on charge of inciting violent anti-government incidents.
1989

35 year old regime in Paraguay overthrown in military coup. Six Jesuit priests, their housekeeper, and her 15-year-old daughter are tortured and murdered in El Salvador. US troops invade Panama to overthrow regime of general Noriega.[ They accuse him of drug trafficking].

1990

In elections in Nicaragua, US-backed coalition under Violeta Chamorro defeats the Sandinista government.
1992

President of Brazil resigns as impeachment proceedings begin against him, [on charges of corruption].
1993

Zapatista National Liberation Army leads rebellion of Indian groups in state of Chiapas,
Mexico.
1994

In Ecuador, Land Development Law is suspended after widespread protests by indigenous population, who claim that it removed their grazing and watering rights.

Sources: 'Chronology of the 20th Century' (1995) Waller & Rowell and selected filmographies.
