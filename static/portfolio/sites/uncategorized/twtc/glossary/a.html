Auteur

A notion that an individual can be identified as the creative author of a film. It is used in various ways. The usage developed by the French 1950s Cahiers du cinèma journal tends to privilege critics in identifying distinctive themes and style in commercial filmmakers. Alfred Hitchcock has been one of the beneficiaries of this view. More recently, auteur has become the directorial equivalent of stardom, so that filmmakers like Quentin Tarantino, David Fincher or are seen as distinctive artists who can attract audiences in a way parallel to Una Thurman or Brad Pitt. Third Cinema directors are often discussed from an auteur standpoint, notably Ousmane Sembène. However, the Third Cinema approach is more inclined to that described by Sanjinés as 'a film made by the people through' a director.
