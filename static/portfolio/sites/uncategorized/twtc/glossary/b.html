The Bay of Pigs

The Bay of Pigs [April 1961] and the Cuban Missile Crisis [October / November 1962]. Shortly after Fidel Castro and his comrades liberated Cuba the new authorities confiscated US owned sugar plantations [the latter an example of neo-colonial exploitation]. The USA has treated Cuba antagonistically ever since. In 1961 Cuban mercenaries, trained and funded by the CIA attempted to invade Cuba. In response the Cubans developed a military alliance with the Soviet Union. This led to a stand-off in 1962 over Soviet missiles stationed in Cuba, [the equivalent of the US missiles stationed in Turkey and aimed at the USSR]. Both episodes are represented in Alea's 'Memories of Underdevelopment'.



Bollywood

This is the nickname for the Bombay centre of the Hindi language film Industry. It appeared in the 1980s and generally represents the idea of popular, star-laden, musical melodramas which are the industry's staple.



Bourgeoisie

"The class of great capitalists who are now almost exclusively in possession of all means of consumption and of the raw materials and instruments (machines, factories) necessary for their production."

[Engels].

The class of owners relies on the labour of the class of workers to transform raw materials with machinery into commodities they can sell, pocketing the bulk of the proceeds. The petty-bourgeoisie lie between these two classes, not having to live by labour but not owning large-scale capitalist businesses.

The descriptions of classes in colloquial English helps disguise the essentially exploitative relationship between a small [upper] class of wealth holders and a large [working] class who either work or go without.
