Cinema Novo

A movement of radical Brazilian filmmakers that emerged in the early 1960s. The movement shared many concerns with other radical Latin American filmmakers. However, it also tended towards embracing the idea of a personal vision or authorship. "In Cinema Novo, expressive forms are necessarily personal and original …" [Carlos Diegues in Johnson and Stam, 1995]. Its most noted member was the filmmaker Glauber Rocha.



Colonialism

The relatively modern exploitation of societies by the capitalist centre, initially Europe. Earlier empires, like the C15th Spanish, extorted tributes from the subjugated peoples. The modern colonial empires, of which Britain was the model and most successful, were primarily about economic control, including scarce resources and markets. This modern colonialism used a variety of methods for extortion, occupation, direct rule, settlers and rule by proxy. Whatever method used, the primary result was that the dominated society was organised to economically serve the coloniser rather than the indigenous peoples. Colonialism develops into Imperialism.



Continuity Editing

A set of conventions developed in early Hollywood, which control the organisation of screen space. The primary purpose is to allow the viewer to concentrate on character and story. Contemporary films often 'bend' these conventions, but broadly speaking, these films still preserve continuity, so that viewers are emotionally caught up in stories rather than positioned to dispassionately judge them.
