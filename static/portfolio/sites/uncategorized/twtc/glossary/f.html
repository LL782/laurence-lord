FEPACI

Fedération Panafricaine des Cinéastes. Created in 1969 in Algiers at the Festival Panafricain de la Culture. There had been an earlier association, Le Groupe Africain du Cinéma, but FEPACI encouraged the association of filmmaker sin national organisations. Its aims are set out in the extracts from the manifesto. It achieved some impact on the Franco-US monopoly in Africa, but it did not success in developed a completely independent production and exhibition sector.



FESPACO

Festival Panafricain du Cinéma à Ouagadougou. Festival Panafricain du Cinéma à Ouagadougou take place in Burkina Faso. Opening in 1969 this film festival has become a major cultural event. It takes place every two years and provides an opportunity for seeing African films and for African filmmakers to meet and organise. The awards are an important recognition of the work of filmmakers.



Francophone

A term for describing the area of Africa colonised by the French and, to a degree, still dominated by French interests. The dependence of many film productions on French finance and technology is an illustration of this. The term Lusophone applies to the areas colonised by the Portuguese.
