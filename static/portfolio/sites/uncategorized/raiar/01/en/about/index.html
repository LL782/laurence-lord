Welcome

Welcome to Raiar. We run courses and events where participants are invited to partake in a collective intelligence whose interest is in the relationship between human existence and the rest of the environment. We encourage involvement from both spiritual and scientific practitioners. Our inspirations, our teachers and our students come from a diverse range of ecological and pedagogical fields. The fruits of our programs are cultivated where Buddhist teachings based on compassion and freedom combine with scientific teachings based on sustainable living. Read more about our visions and models below.

We are working to set up a centre here in Rio Grande do Norte, Brazil, where the academic, technological and spiritual practices with which we interest ourselves are put into practice, demonstrated live and made available to our programs' participants. Active Contemplation is one practice that is key to our methods. At this centre we cultivate a space to allow both teachers and students to engage in active contemplation for increased intelligence and deeper thinking. Read more about active contemplation below.

Visions and Models

For the Benefit of All

Raiar means to radiate. We bring together people related through a drive to benefit the world, the environment and all those existing in it. At the centre of Raiar ideas from very different origins converge and what radiates is fuel for creative, educational and environmental projects.

Our ultimate intention is that our actions will benefit everyone, not just those around us currently but those to come in future generations all around the globe. We hope that the fruit of our collective's intelligence will intellectually and spiritually enhance our participants and naturally benefit their communities and the global community as a whole.

Buddhism, Science and Action

The twentieth-century disengagement of ethics from scientific inquiry ... has been disastrous for humanity. ...We are now faced with the challenge to evolve spiritually so that we can adapt to the rapid changes in the social and natural environment so that we may survive and possibly flourish as never before in history.
B. Alan Wallace "Buddhism and Science"

Through our programs we present, discuss and teach scientific and spiritual theory along with practical environmental techniques. Amongst our teachers are ordained Buddhist lamas, federal university professors and practical experts in the fields of permaculture and ecology.

In the face of the contemporary environmental challenges we recognise the importance of a) knowing what needs to be done, b) having the technical ability to go about doing it, and c) cultivating the mind-state that results in actually taking appropriate action.

Active Contemplation

Buddhist wisdom can be seen as a science inspired by compassion and freedom. This is one reason why it is compatible with environmental sciences that seek to bring human existence into a healthier balance with the environment. Aside from this, Buddhism offers exercises that have been developed over thousands of years to optimize everything we do, mentally and spiritually.

Through mind-training techniques an individual can improve their ability to concentrate and focus the mind, leading to a greater capacity for deep-contemplation and undistracted thinking. Such techniques also increase emotional control and peace of mind.

We design our programs to allow participants to engage in active contemplation at a level appropriate to their individual level of experience.
